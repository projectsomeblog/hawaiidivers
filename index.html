What to Wear and Pack for a Shark Cage Diving Trip

Shark cage diving is an exhilarating adventure that allows you to get up close and personal with one of the ocean’s most feared predators. Whether you are planning a trip to South Africa, Australia, or anywhere else where this thrilling activity is offered, knowing what to wear and what to pack is essential to ensure a safe and comfortable experience. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you prepare for your Hawaii Divers adventure.

Clothing: Dress for Comfort and Safety

When it comes to shark cage diving, your clothing needs to balance warmth, comfort, and practicality. Most operators provide wetsuits, especially for colder waters, but it’s important to understand what you should wear underneath and for the journey to and from the dive site.

  1. Wetsuits and Thermal Layers: Even if a wetsuit is provided, wearing a thermal layer or a swimsuit underneath can add extra comfort and insulation. Neoprene wetsuits vary in thickness, usually between 3mm to 5mm, which is suitable for most temperate waters. In colder regions like South Africa during winter, a thicker wetsuit or an extra thermal layer can help prevent hypothermia.
  2. Lightweight, Quick-Drying Clothing: For the time you spend on the boat, lightweight and quick-drying clothing is ideal. Think shorts or swim trunks, T-shirts, and a light long-sleeve shirt for sun protection. Avoid heavy fabrics like jeans, which can become uncomfortable if they get wet.
  3. Footwear: Non-slip, waterproof shoes or sandals are recommended for boarding the boat. Boats can be wet and slippery, so safety should come first. Flip-flops are usually discouraged unless you are confident in your balance.

Essential Accessories

  1. Hat and Sunglasses: A wide-brimmed hat and polarized sunglasses are essential for sun protection while waiting on the deck. The sun reflecting off the water can be intense, so protecting your eyes is just as important as your skin.
  2. Gloves and Beanies: For colder waters, thermal gloves and a beanie or neoprene hood can make a significant difference in comfort. Many operators provide these items, but bringing your own ensures a perfect fit.
  3. Waterproof Camera or GoPro: Capturing the action is a highlight of shark cage diving. Waterproof cameras or GoPros with wrist straps are ideal for hands-free filming. Make sure your camera has a secure attachment to avoid losing it in the water.

Packing Essentials

  1. Towel and Change of Clothes: After the dive, you’ll want a dry towel and a fresh set of clothes. Even with a wetsuit, you are likely to get wet from splashing water and ocean spray. A comfortable change of clothes helps you stay warm and enjoy the ride back.
  2. Snacks and Water: Shark cage diving trips often last several hours, including boat rides to and from the dive site. Pack water and light snacks to stay hydrated and energized throughout the day. Avoid glass containers for safety reasons.
  3. Motion Sickness Remedies: If you are prone to seasickness, bring medication or natural remedies such as ginger. Even calm waters can get choppy, and nausea can spoil your adventure if unprepared.
  4. Sunscreen and Lip Balm: Saltwater can amplify sun exposure. Use waterproof sunscreen and lip balm with SPF to prevent burns, even on overcast days. Apply before boarding the boat and reapply after the dive if needed.
  5. Personal Items: Don’t forget essentials like identification, money, and any necessary travel documents. Store them in a waterproof pouch to prevent damage from splashes.

Final Tips

Comfort, safety, and preparedness are key to enjoying your shark cage diving experience. Dress in layers, bring waterproof gear, and ensure you have essentials to stay warm, dry, and sun-protected. With the right clothing and packing strategy, you can focus on the thrill of encountering sharks in their natural habitat and make unforgettable memories.
